# Prior Work and Inspiration

VoxelCivitas is part of a broader research lineage around artificial intelligence, agent-based modeling, multi-agent systems, and Minecraft-compatible virtual environments.

This document is not a full literature review. It records the early reference points that shape the project direction.

## Microsoft Project Malmo

Microsoft Project Malmo showed that Minecraft could be used as a platform for AI experimentation and research. It provided a way for agents to perceive, act, and learn inside a rich block-based world with spatial structure, resources, and repeatable tasks.

VoxelCivitas is inspired by that idea, but it aims at a different layer of the research workflow:

- experiment design,
- simulation orchestration,
- agent configuration,
- structured data collection,
- dashboard visualization,
- reproducible run comparison,
- and academic-style report generation.

VoxelCivitas is independent and is not affiliated with Microsoft or Project Malmo.

## Related Minecraft-Based Research Platforms

### MineRL

MineRL advanced research on learning from human demonstrations and reinforcement learning in Minecraft-like tasks. It is relevant to VoxelCivitas because it treats block-based environments as serious AI research settings rather than entertainment-only spaces.

### MineDojo

MineDojo explored open-ended embodied agents in Minecraft environments. Its emphasis on generalist agents, large task spaces, and embodied interaction is relevant to long-running VoxelCivitas experiments.

### Voyager

Voyager demonstrated an approach to lifelong skill acquisition for agents acting in Minecraft. It is relevant to future VoxelCivitas work around memory, tool use, planning, and progressive capability development.

## Generative Agents

Generative agent research has shown how agents with memory, reflection, planning, and social interaction can produce believable emergent behavior. VoxelCivitas is interested in applying related ideas to spatial, resource-constrained, instrumented virtual worlds.

## Agent-Based Modeling

Agent-based modeling provides a conceptual foundation for studying how local rules and individual behaviors can produce system-level patterns. VoxelCivitas aims to make some of these ideas testable inside interactive virtual environments with structured metrics.

## Multi-Agent Systems Research

Multi-agent systems research informs the project's interest in cooperation, competition, coordination, communication, governance, and group behavior. VoxelCivitas will use this area as a guide for future experiment templates and evaluation metrics.
